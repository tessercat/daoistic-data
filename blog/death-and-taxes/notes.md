使有什伯之器 probably means something like,
"Providing people with tech that produces ten times more",
and probably in an agricultural context.

使民 is something like *appoint a citizen*,
and there are two of these statements,
so I guess that
in Laozi's libertarian utopia,
government serves two functions,
to remember the dead
and to keep track of tax obligations.

復結繩 is something like
*remit talking knots*,
referring to a method of using knotted cords
to keep track of (I assume) tax obligations.

往來 is *come and go* or *to and fro*,
the constant frantic motion
of the modern world.
