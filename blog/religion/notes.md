This poem
makes more sense to me
with 王 as 士 on the "priest" end of the 士 spectrum.
It becomes a reaction
to evangelists who would make humans divine.

The more I read this book,
the less it looks like
a book for kings and other worldly leaders,
and the more it looks like
a handbook of effective
(personal and)
social reform.
Whoever wrote these poems
had seen a lot of woke evangelists get killed,
or worse,
set their hand to
killing and oppressing others
in the name of some holy crusade.

My guess is that Laozi lived
in the wake of
[Mozi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mozi "Mozi on Wikipedia")
and his righteous movement,
and gradually,
some of the vocabulary
may have changed
as his (or her?) poetry
was re-purposed by people
reacting to the power and presumption
of (other) kings and aristocrats.
