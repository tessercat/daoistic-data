常 appears in two
of the poem's couplets.
In the first,
it's usually translated
as something like *eternal*,
but according to
Kroll's 非 entry,
非常 is something like
*extraordinary*,
and I prefer that here
because it lets me frame
the subject of the poem nicely
without naming it.

In the second 常 couplet,
I prefer it as
something like
a habitual mode.

This poem
is very good advice
on how to read the book itself.
Stay neutral
regarding interpretation
until subtle relationships emerge,
then commit to key terms,
and everything flows from there.

The _Dao De Jing_ uses 謂
to name certain important concepts,
as it does here for 玄.

Other named 玄 binomials
are in 6 (玄牝),
in 10, 51 and 65 (玄德),
and in 56 (玄同).

I'm committed to
Xuanpin,
Xuande,
Xuantong,
deeply female,
deeply moral,
deeply ambivalent.
