# Art

大道汎兮其可左右
Dao is temperamental. It could go either way.

萬物恃之而生而不辭功成不名有
Every creature has a role to play,
but it creates us and doesn't explain it to us.
It's a skilled artist that doesn't sign its work.
衣養萬物而不為主常無欲可名於小
It feeds and shelters us,
but it doesn't control us.
Looking at it objectively,
you could say it's insignificant.
萬物歸焉而不為主可名為大
We all return to it
but we have no say in the matter.
You could say it's great.

以其終不自為大故能成其大
In the end,
it's not trying to be great,
and that's what lets it do such great work.


# Notes

https://ctext.org/dao-de-jing#n11625

Laozi on Dao as a great artist. 道德經 34.

This is one of a few poems
that address 大道, the great way.
Here it's a great artist.

常無欲 is an expression from
[chapter 1](https://ctext.org/dao-de-jing#n11592 "ctext link")
that explains to how to 以觀其妙,
observe its subtleties,
as opposed to 常有欲以觀其徼,
the subjective state of longing
that lets us observe how to describe it.
