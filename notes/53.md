If you asked me for advice on
how to walk the great way,
I would offer only this warning.
使我介然有知
行於大道
唯施是畏

The great way is too esoteric
and people prefer [direct route].
大道甚夷而民好徑

Your court is too [ru-ish],
the fields are too weedy,
the granaries are too empty.
朝甚除
田甚蕪
倉甚虛

You wear silk robes
and carry a sharp sword.
You stuff your face
and line your pockets.
That makes you a rich thief.
服文綵
帶利劍
厭飲食
財貨有餘
是謂盜夸

Definitely a dead end.
非道也哉

# Notes

This is one of two poems
in the _Daodejing_
to use the term 大道.

It's literally "main street",
and Heshanggong commentary
adds a related Laozi saying
that goes something like,
"In public life,
I follow main street,
In private life,
I conform by doing nothing."

In [chapter 18](/studies/18),
a public figure
who strays too far from it
gets preachy,
and here,
the author explains
why it's a good idea
to avoid public life
when tyrants rule.
