# Truth to Power

使我介然有知行於大道
唯施是畏
大道甚夷
而民好徑
When people ask me for my opinion on the current state of great Dao,
I start by reminding myself
that great Dao is very crude,
but that neighbours appreciate candour.

朝甚除
田甚蕪
倉甚虛
I say that
court is too pretentious,
fields too overgrown,
granaries too empty.
服文綵
帶利劍
厭飲食
財貨有餘
I remind people
that they wear silk robes
and carry sharp swords,
that they stuff their faces
and line their pockets.
是謂盜夸
非道也哉
I call them a den of thieves
and I tell them that
they're going the wrong way.


# Notes

It's hard to know
what to make of 除
as a description of 朝,
but I take it as
the opposite of Dao's 夷,
and my solution
hinges on it.

除 usually describes
the completion of a mourning period,
but it also describes roads,
and when it does so,
it's a trail that's been blazed,
or a highway maintained.

So I think Laozi is telling fellow citizens (民),
in a crude, uncivilized way (夷),
that the court's modern, civilized ways (除) are corrupt,
and run counter to Laozi's broken-down old 大道
and won't last.
